Our approach

Two analysis tiers. Zero unit cost for passive users.

Most LLM-powered media tools burn margin on every article view. We don't. A free local tier handles 90 % of routine browsing; a deep AI tier fires only when the user asks for it.

Trust over time

Source credibility isn't static. We track the trend.

Each major outlet's trust rating updates over time. Signal Lens stores the year-by-year history so users see whether a source is improving or eroding, not just where it stands today.

CNN's composite trust score declined steadily across audits over eight years. Sources: Reuters Institute Digital News Report (2018–2024), Gallup Trust in Mass Media (annual), MBFC + AllSides rating-update logs. The same trend dataset ships with Signal Lens for 28 major outlets — visible as a sparkline in the popup.

A reader who has been told CNN is "trustworthy" needs to know that trust has slipped 10 points. A reader who has been told Fox News is "biased" needs to know if anything is changing. Signal Lens surfaces both.
